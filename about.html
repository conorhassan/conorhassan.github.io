About Me

I am a PhD student at the QUT Centre for Data Science in Brisbane, Australia, working with Distinguished Professor Kerrie Mengersen on methods for Bayesian federated learning and differentially private generative models. My thesis is currently under internal review.

From May to November 2024, I relocated to Melbourne to complete an internship with the machine learning research team at Amazon.

My research interests include variational inference, generative models, federated and distributed algorithms, latent-variable models, and Bayesian deep learning.

Previously, I worked as a research data scientist at WearOptimo, a biotechnology startup developing microwearable devices, and completed internships in quantitative trading, consulting, and at a national scientific institute. I have also worked as a research assistant and tutored seven courses in mathematics and statistics.

I am well-versed in using automatic differentiation libraries like JAX and PyTorch, as well as probabilistic programming libraries such as NumPyro, PyMC, Pyro, and Stan.
